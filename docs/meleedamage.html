Playing a Melee DPS class in World of Warcraft

Playing a melee damage dealing class in World of Warcraft has its advantages and disadvantages. On the one hand, melee classes have the advantage of being able to deal significant damage up close and personal, which can be very satisfying in battles. Additionally, some melee classes have unique abilities that allow them to mitigate damage or control the battlefield. On the other hand, playing a melee class also means being in the thick of battle, which can be risky and require more attention to positioning and movement.

Melee classes may also have a harder time with certain bosses or enemies that require more mobility or ranged attacks. Furthermore, as the game evolves and new content is added, the balance between classes can shift, meaning that certain melee classes may be more or less effective than others at different points in time. Ultimately, the choice to play a melee class comes down to personal preference and playstyle.

Melee DPS Classes and Specializations Include:

  • Arms/Fury Warrior
  • Unholy/Frost Death Knight
  • Retribution Paladin
  • Survival Hunter
  • Enhancement Shaman
  • Assassination/Subtlety/Outlaw Rogue
  • Feral Druid
  • Windwalker Monk
  • Havoc Demon Hunter

Class Recommendation for New Players:

  • Arms/Fury Warrior
  • Frost Death Knight
  • Havoc Demon Hunter

The classes listed above have a straight forward play style with little complexity needed in terms of gameplay in order to be successful. I suggest new players that want to play melee damage dealers choose between the classes and specializations listed here.
